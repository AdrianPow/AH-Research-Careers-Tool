Allied Health Research Careers Development Framework — Self-Assessment Tool

Office of the Chief Allied Health Officer

Purpose of the tool

This tool helps allied health professionals identify their current level of research skills and experience against the five domains of the Allied Health Research Careers Development Framework. It is designed to be used independently or in collaboration with your leader or mentor to support career planning and professional development.

How to use it

The assessment contains 61 competency statements grouped into five domains. For each statement, choose the rating that best reflects your current level of research capability and experience. When you have finished, a personalised capability profile and development report is generated, which you can print or save as a PDF.

Your responses are saved automatically in this browser only — nothing is sent anywhere. You can close the page and return later to continue.

Understanding your self-assessment profile

This self-assessment provides an overview of your current research capabilities. As you review your results, it is important to consider your scores as a baseline for professional development and career planning, rather than a definitive performance assessment.

Before reviewing your specific domain feedback, please consider the following context:

Variation across domains is expected: It is common to demonstrate different capabilities and proficiency levels across the five domains. Research skills develop over time and are shaped by your role, interests, and opportunities.

Different roles require different capability profiles: The framework covers a wide range of clinical, research, and leadership roles. Not all roles require advanced skills in every domain. Having a different profile does not make one role more or less valuable.

Scores reflect opportunity as well as capability: The development of capabilities is influenced by the context of your workplace and the training and development opportunities available to you. A lower score often reflects less opportunity to practise and demonstrate those specific skills — not a lack of ability or potential.

Applying your results: Your profile provides a snapshot of your existing strengths and establishes a baseline for your development journey. If your capabilities currently align well with your service needs and role requirements, this tool aims to provide you with ideas for continuing to apply your skills and/or communicating your capabilities to others. If you are looking to advance or broaden your research capabilities for current or future roles, your results can support conversations with your manager or mentor to identify targeted training, new opportunities, and a structured professional development plan.

Next steps: actioning your results

As you reflect on your capability profile, remember that it is not necessary or expected to achieve high proficiency across every domain. Professional development is a targeted process, and you do not need to address every area for improvement simultaneously.

Focus your efforts: Select one to three specific development areas to prioritise. Ensure these focus areas are relevant to the direction you want your career to take, your current role requirements, and the broader strategic needs of your service.

Set SMART goals: Translate your chosen development areas into goals that are:

  • Specific — clear and unambiguous, so you know exactly what you are working towards.
  • Measurable — quantifiable or observable, allowing you to track progress and know when the goal has been achieved.
  • Achievable — realistic and attainable within your current role, resources, and time constraints.
  • Relevant — aligned with your research career aspirations and the operational needs of your organisation.
  • Time-bound — tied to a clearly defined timeline, including specific starting and target completion dates.

Collaborate with leadership: Discuss your self-assessment and development plan with your line manager, allied health leader, or a trusted research mentor. Collaborative planning will provide additional perspectives, help refine your strategies, and ensure you can agree on appropriate and meaningful opportunities to contribute your unique capabilities to your service.

Further development ideas are available on the Allied Health Research Careers Development Framework website.
