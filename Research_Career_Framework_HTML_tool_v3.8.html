Allied Health Research Careers Development Framework Self-Assessment Tool

Purpose of the Tool

To assist allied health professionals to identify their current level of research skills and experience against the 5 domains of the Research Career Development Framework, and to identify areas for future development.

How to Use the Tool

This tool consists of 5 domains. Under each domain there are a number of competency statements. Please select the rating that best reflects your current level of research capability/experience for each statement. Once you have completed all sections, a summary report will be generated.

Rating Scale

Level Description
1 - No proficiency May have some awareness of the area but no experience or skill in the area and no ability to demonstrate capability.
2 - Emerging proficiency Gaining experience in the described knowledge and skills and starting to apply that knowledge to research activities and tasks. Requires support or mentoring to achieve outcomes.
3 - Consolidating proficiency Furthering skills and knowledge and can complete research activities or tasks independently. Requires guidance to deliver complex or advanced levels of activity or tasks.
4 - Advanced proficiency Becoming an expert in skill and knowledge required to demonstrate behaviour or activity. Able to apply skills and knowledge across a range of contexts and integrate expertise across domains. Able to support others in learning and applying knowledge, skills, and behaviour.
5 - Expert proficiency Acknowledged leader with in-depth knowledge and demonstrated strategic leadership in the area of knowledge, skill, or behaviour. Integrates, evaluates, and adapts knowledge, skills, or behaviour at an advanced level.

Understanding Your Self-Assessment Profile

This self-assessment provides a comprehensive overview of your current research capabilities. As you review your results, it is important to view your scores as a baseline for professional growth and career planning, rather than a definitive performance measure.

Before reviewing your specific domain feedback, please consider the following context regarding your results:

Variation across domains is expected: It is common to demonstrate different capabilities and proficiency levels across the five domains. Expertise is typically focused within specific areas of practice, and a varied capability profile is a standard reflection of an individualised and developing career.

Different roles require different capability profiles: The framework encompasses a broad spectrum of clinical and research positions. Consequently, different roles naturally require different capability and proficiency levels. Advanced skills in certain domains are simply not required for some role types. Differences in capability profiles do not imply that any particular profile or role is less capable or valuable than another.

Scores reflect opportunity alongside ability: The development of capabilities is heavily influenced by the context in which you work and the training and development opportunities available to you. A lower score often highlights a lack of previous opportunity to practice and demonstrate those specific skills within your service, rather than a reflection of your inherent aptitude or potential.

Applying your results: Your profile provides a snapshot of your existing strengths and establishes a baseline for your development journey. If your capabilities currently align well with your service needs and role requirements, this tool serves to validate your practice.

If you are looking to advance or broaden your scope, your results can be used collaboratively with your manager or mentor to identify targeted training, navigate new opportunities, and formulate a structured professional development plan.

Next Steps: Actioning Your Results

As you reflect on your capability profile, please remember that it is not necessary or expected to achieve high proficiency across every domain. Professional development is a targeted process, and you do not need to address every area for improvement simultaneously.

To effectively translate your self-assessment into actionable growth, consider the following next steps:

Focus your efforts: Select one to three specific development areas to prioritise. Ensure these focus areas are relevant to the direction you want your career to take, your current role requirements, and the broader strategic needs of your service.

Set SMART goals: To create a focused and relevant professional growth plan, translate your chosen development areas into SMART goals. Ensure each goal is:

  • Specific: Clear and unambiguous, so you know exactly what you are working towards.
  • Measurable: Quantifiable or observable, allowing you to track progress and know when the goal has been achieved.
  • Achievable: Realistic and attainable within your current role, resources, and time constraints.
  • Relevant: Aligned with your research career aspirations and the operational needs of your organisation.
  • Time-bound: Tied to a clearly defined timeline, including specific starting and target completion dates.

Collaborate with leadership: We strongly encourage you to discuss your self-assessment and development plan with your line manager, allied health leader, or a trusted research mentor. Collaborative planning will provide additional perspectives, help refine your strategies, and ensure you can agree on appropriate and meaningful opportunities to contribute your unique capabilities to your service.
